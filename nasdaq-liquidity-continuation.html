NASDAQ Liquidity Continuation Framework
Gate 1 Liquidity → Gate 2 Trend → Gate 3 NY Confirmation → Gate 4 Dynamic Exit · Brand-new, self-contained · Daily backtest 2014–present
Run Backtest:
How it works — Gate 1 (Liquidity): a daily LiquidityScore (−5..+5) from a weighted, z-scored panel of Fed/ECB/BOJ balance sheets, reverse repo, TGA, the 10Y-2Y curve, DXY, HY credit spreads, NFCI, HYG/LQD, VIX/VIX3M/VVIX — score > +2 = BULLISH, < −2 = BEARISH, else no directional bias. Gate 2 (Trend): a 0-100 TrendScore from ADX, Hurst exponent, ATR percentile, directional momentum, breadth trend, VWAP conviction and VIX term structure — >70 = VALID, with a risk tier (LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH). Gate 3 (NY Confirmation): a weighted vote of futures/DXY/yield/breadth moves classifies LONG/SHORT/INVALID; true NYSE TICK/A-D/TRIN have no free daily proxy and abstain (disclosed, not faked). Entry: Gate1 BULLISH + Gate2 VALID + Gate3 LONG → long (mirror for short). Gate 4 (Dynamic Exit): a ContinuationScore re-evaluated every bar — >70 stay in full size, 50-70 reduce to half, <40 close — backed by a standing ATR-multiple stop. Fills are next-bar-open (no lookahead); a stop can still trigger intrabar since it was placed before that bar opened. Every threshold lives in js/nasdaqConfig.js — nothing here is a black box.
