System Logic: Gate 1 checks the overnight Asia session at ~09:00 UTC — if NAS100 price is in the top 40% of the overnight range, directional bias is LONG (bottom 40% = SHORT). Gate 2 checks London momentum at ~12:00 UTC — if London has moved >0.10% in the same direction as Gate 1, both gates confirm and a trade fires at the 13:00 UTC bar (≈09:25 ET). Stop is 0.5% from entry, max account risk 1.8% (3.6× position leverage). Exit at EOD (20:00 UTC) or stop-out.

Monte Carlo Simulation (IID Bootstrap — 500 runs)
Resamples trade returns with replacement 500 times to show the distribution of possible outcomes, including max-drawdown risk and per-trade EV/Kelly edge quality. The fan chart shows p10–p90 of simulated equity paths. Statistical significance comes from the same resampled paths — the fraction with Sharpe ≤ 0 is a bootstrap p-value for whether the edge is distinguishable from noise.
Block Bootstrap — Autocorrelation-Preserving
Samples consecutive blocks of trades to preserve autocorrelation and streak effects. Unlike IID bootstrap, this captures multi-trade dependency. Compare its p10/p90 range against the IID result above — a wider range here signals clustering risk.
Validation — Train / Test & Walk-Forward
Train/test splits the 5yr period chronologically (first 60% = in-sample, last 40% = out-of-sample). Walk-forward evaluates each calendar year independently.
