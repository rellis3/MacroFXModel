Layer 1 — Confluence Priority Mode

What it does: Instead of a min-score gate that silently drops trades, this mode scores every candidate zone against all enabled modules, sorts them by the number of module hits (highest first), then simulates only the top N for that day.

Why it helps: On a given day you might have 40 fib levels across the Asia and Monday ranges. Most have zero or one module hit. The top 3–5 usually cluster structural levels, round numbers, PDH/PDL, and session open ranges — those are the genuinely interesting zones. Trading only the top 5 by density focuses capital on the strongest setups and eliminates noise trades that happened to be near a level with no additional confluence.

Stacking: Works with all other filters. The level filter (tight/confluence/all) still applies before scoring — Priority mode selects from whatever survives the level filter.

Key difference from min-score: Min-score uses a weighted percentage gate (e.g. "must hit ≥50% of module weight"). Priority mode is rank-based — it always produces exactly N trades per day regardless of absolute score, which avoids low-signal days producing zero trades and high-signal days producing many. Set N=1 for the single best zone per day.

Layer 3A — WaveTrend OB/OS Threshold Gate

What it does: At the exact M1 bar where price first touches a zone, computes WT1 (WaveTrend 1, the core oscillator inside VuManchu Cipher B) and checks whether momentum is exhausted in the direction of the trade.

Formula: hlc3 = (H+L+C)/3 → ESA = EMA(hlc3, n1) → D = EMA(|hlc3−ESA|, n1) → CI = (hlc3−ESA)/(0.015×D) → WT1 = EMA(CI, n2). Range approx −100 to +100.

Entry rules:
 · BUY at support zone: WT1 must be ≤ buy threshold (default −40 = oversold)
 · SELL at resistance zone: WT1 must be ≥ sell threshold (default +40 = overbought)

Why this matters: Without WT confirmation, a zone touch includes both: (a) price pausing at a level because momentum is exhausted — likely to reverse, and (b) price blowing through a level with strong momentum — likely to continue. WT confirmation filters for case (a) only by requiring momentum to already be stretched at the touch bar.

Settings: n1=10 / n2=21 matches the default VuManchu Cipher B settings and the live bot's compute_wt1() in bot/utils/indicators.py. Thresholds of ±40 are permissive (captures more trades); ±53 or ±60 are the standard OB/OS levels used in VuManchu.

Layer 3B — WaveTrend Divergence Gate

What it does: At the touch bar, looks back N M1 bars within the trade window to find the prior price extreme, then checks whether WT1 agrees with that extreme or diverges.

Bullish divergence (BUY): Price at touch bar makes a lower low than any bar in the lookback window, BUT WT1 at the touch bar is higher than WT1 was at the prior low. Meaning: price made a new low but momentum did not — sellers are exhausted.

Bearish divergence (SELL): Price at touch bar makes a higher high than any bar in the lookback window, BUT WT1 at the touch bar is lower than WT1 was at the prior high. Meaning: price made a new high but momentum did not — buyers are exhausted.

Why it's stronger than OB/OS: OB/OS simply says "momentum is stretched right now." Divergence says "momentum was stronger the last time price was here — this new extreme is weaker." That relative weakening is the classic VuManchu red/green diamond signal and is a much higher-probability reversal signal than an absolute threshold alone.

Note: Divergence requires at least 5 trade-window bars before the touch bar to have a meaningful lookback. Trades touched on the very first bars of the London open will be skipped if the lookback window is too short. Increase the lookback (default 30 bars = 30 minutes) if you want to capture more divergence patterns but accept wider context.

Stacking All Three Layers

The three layers are independent and can be combined:
 · Priority mode narrows the zone list to the highest-confluence candidates
 · OB/OS gate checks that momentum is already stretched when price arrives at the zone
 · Divergence gate confirms that this push into the zone is weaker than the last one

Running all three is the most selective filter — it will dramatically reduce trade count but should concentrate the highest-probability setups. Start by testing each layer in isolation, then compare combined results against the baseline (all layers off) using the Analytics engine.
