Honest Forecast Harness

Re-tests the daily vol/range forecast under honest assumptions — real costs, breach-and-reclaim fills, and a true in-sample / out-of-sample split — and answers the central question: at the forecast exhaustion band (open ± HL₇₅), should you fade (mean-revert), follow (continuation), or let the regime decide? All three run side by side so degradation and the fade-vs-follow edge are both visible.

Configuration

last 40% of dates = out-of-sample
exit otherwise = mark-to-close
round-trip spread+comm
How to read it. Each instrument shows the three entry hypotheses. fade = current behaviour (sell the up-band / buy the down-band). follow = breakout (buy the up-band / sell the down-band). regime = follow in a trending regime, fade in RANGE.
The number that matters is OOS Sharpe on a large OOS trade count. A mode that only looks good in-sample, or wins OOS on <30 trades, is noise. The harness deducts real costs and (by default) requires breach-and-reclaim, so these numbers are deliberately harsher — and more honest — than the original dashboards.
