Yield Spread Z-Score — strategy logic
Signal
Spread = US 2Y yield (GS2) − local short rate (JP/DE/GB/AU OECD short rate, or CA/CH for the USD-base pairs). A rolling z-score of that spread is computed daily; when |z| exceeds the pair's threshold, the day is "live" and biased LONG (z > +thresh) or SHORT (z < −thresh). USD-quote pairs (EURUSD/GBPUSD/AUDUSD) default to a higher threshold than USD-base pairs (USDJPY/USDCAD/USDCHF) to offset negative RR below it.
Entry
The Asia session (00:00–05:59 UTC) body range (no wicks) anchors Fibonacci extension levels projected outside it (0.25/0.75/1.25/1.5/2.0 × range). During the entry window after 06:00 UTC, the first touch of each untraded level fills at that exact price. Reward (to the Asia range edge) must be ≥0.8× the risk (to the next level out).
Exit
TP = opposite Asia range edge (mean reversion). SL = one Fib step further from the range (0.25×range beyond entry). Stop is checked before target each bar. Unresolved trades are closed at the open of the first bar ≥22:00 UTC (EXPIRED).
v1 limitations: no spread/commission cost, no notional sizing, all yield legs are monthly series forward-filled to daily (so the z-score updates in monthly steps, not daily), one entry per level/day, no economic-event filter, and only USDJPY's direction sign has been validated against live results — use the ⚙ Pairs panel's Invert checkbox to test the flip on any pair.
