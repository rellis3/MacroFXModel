🌊 Global Liquidity — how to read it

What this is for

A liquidity-driven macro read for FX. It nowcasts global money conditions (the Fed, ECB, BoJ, PBoC and private/shadow liquidity), decides which macro regime you're in, and proposes a cross-sectional FX book — long the currencies whose liquidity is expanding fastest, short the weakest. It is a slow, weekly decision aid, not an intraday signal scanner.

How to use it

Check it about once a week (the inputs only update weekly). Read it top-down: Regime → Impulse → Book. Most weeks the right action is to do little — the edge is in being early on the few real turns and sizing to them, not in trading often. Expect ~1–3 position changes per week, very lumpy; a regime flip re-expresses the whole book at once.

Reading each part

  • Regime bannerOne of four states from liquidity impulse × growth. Its line tells you the lean (e.g. REFLATION = long risk / sell USD & funders).
  • Risk gateThe override. When credit spreads or vol spike it turns red and cuts gross — respect it regardless of how bullish liquidity looks.
  • GLI LevelWhere liquidity is (z-score). Regime context.
  • Liquidity ImpulseThe timing signal. 13-week rate-of-change — markets trade the turn, not the level. Positive & rising = tailwind for risk.
  • Cycle PhaseWhere you sit in the ~65-month liquidity cycle (Trough → Rising → Peak → Falling).
  • ConvictionHow strongly to size — impulse strength × growth. Low conviction → smaller book.
  • Per-currency barsEach currency's own liquidity impulse. Green = expanding, red = draining. The book trades the spread between them.
  • Target FX bookLong the top / short the bottom by liquidity-impulse spread. Weights are gross-normalised (sum of absolute weights = 1); scale to your own risk.
