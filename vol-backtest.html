Strategy modes
Reversal
Waits for price to reach the 75th-pct daily range extreme (HL75) then fades back toward the median close (OC_med). Entry is a limit order at the edge. R:R ≈ 0.5–0.7:1, so needs a high fill-to-win rate (~60%+).
BULL → SELL limit at open+HL75, TP open+OC_med
BEAR → BUY limit at open−HL75, TP open−OC_med
RANGE → fade whichever extreme fills first
Rev-HL50 ★
Open-anchored mean reversion. When price hits the projected HL50 (median daily range), fade it back to open. SL at HL75. R:R ≈ 3.8:1. Break-even only ~21%. No regime filter — takes first HL50 hit each day. Confirmed by chart analysis: ~66% of HL50 hits return to open.
High → SELL limit at open+HL50, TP = open, SL = open+HL75
Low → BUY limit at open−HL50, TP = open, SL = open−HL75
Rev-50
Dynamic-anchor reversal. Tracks the developing intraday extreme bar-by-bar; once it's established (≥30% of HL50), fades a HL50 pullback from that pivot back to the pivot. R:R ≈ 3.8:1. More complex, requires an intraday swing to form first.
Swing high → BUY at runHigh−HL50, TP runHigh, SL runHigh−HL75
Swing low → SELL at runLow+HL50, TP runLow, SL runLow+HL75
Momentum
Trades with the regime direction toward HL75. Entry near the open (or small pullback), TP at HL75, SL at OC_med in opposite direction. R:R ≈ 2.5–3:1, break-even ~25–30%. Skips RANGE days.
Pullback — how far price must dip before entry (0 = at open).
Mo-SL — stop loss as multiple of OC_med.
Both
Runs Reversal + Momentum simultaneously on every day. Two rows per day in the results. Useful for seeing whether the strategies are complementary — check the Leg filter to isolate each.
Mom-50
Enter with regime once price breaks HL50, target HL75, symmetric 1:1 stop. R:R 1:1, needs >50% win rate. RANGE days skipped.
BULL → stop-BUY at open+HL50, TP open+HL75
BEAR → stop-SELL at open−HL50, TP open−HL75
SL × — reversal stop loss as a multiple of HL75 beyond the entry level (default ×1.5). Wider SL survives more intraday noise but increases loss size.  |  M1 simulation — when M1 parquet files are present in data/m1/, each day's fill/TP/SL sequence is resolved by walking 1-minute bars, eliminating the D1 high/low ordering ambiguity entirely.
★ Rev-HL50 Strategy Guide Open-anchored mean reversion at the HL50 level
How the trade works
Entry triggerPrice hits open ± HL50 (median daily range level). First hit of the day wins — either side. Take profitOpen — full mean reversion to the daily open. Distance = HL50 ≈ 1.45σ. Stop lossopen ± HL75 — the 75th-pct projected range level. Distance = HL75−HL50 ≈ 0.38σ. R:R~3.8:1 theoretical (1.45σ profit / 0.38σ risk). Actual R:R from backtest: 5.89:1. Break-evenOnly 21% win rate needed to cover losses. Actual: 13.6% fills → profitable because losses are so small. Fill rate~28% of days — only days where the daily range extends to HL50 will generate a trade.
SELL example: EURUSD opens 1.0800. HL50 = +0.37% → entry SELL at 1.0840. TP at 1.0800. SL at HL75 = +0.53% → 1.0857.
Run bar settings for Rev-HL50
Strategy Rev-HL50 ★ — select this in the strategy dropdown. SL × Ignored for Rev-HL50. The SL is always fixed at HL75 regardless of this setting. It applies only to the Reversal strategy. Slope × Controls the EMA slope threshold for BULL/BEAR/RANGE regime classification. Only matters if you use Counter-regime mode (see below). Default ×1 (0.2%) is fine for most runs. Rev mode
Any direction — best baseline. Takes the first HL50 hit each day regardless of regime. Backtest result: Sharpe 4.71, CAGR 49%, Max DD −10.6%.
Counter-regime — on BULL days skip the SELL-from-high (trend likely to push to HL75). On BEAR days skip the BUY-from-low. Trades fewer fills, potentially higher win rate. Run both and compare Sharpe.
Spread Add 0.001–0.002% to simulate realistic bid/ask. With no cost the CAGR is 49%; a small spread tests robustness. Dyn-HL Off — not applicable to Rev-HL50. The entry and TP are fixed at open±HL50 and open.

What to watch when tuning
  • Win rate dropping below 15% with same R:R = edge degrading
  • Sharpe below 2.0 = consider reverting changes
  • Max DD above 20% = over-leveraged or regime breakdown
  • Profit Factor below 1.2 = gross wins barely covering gross losses
Why it works
Price reaching HL50 means the day has used its median expected range in one direction. Mean reversion back to open then becomes the higher-probability path. The tight SL at HL75 keeps losses small even when wrong — the 3.8:1 R:R means you can be wrong 4 times for every win and still break even.
