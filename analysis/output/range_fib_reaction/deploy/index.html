Projects the same Fibonacci grid js/asiaRangeEngine.js uses (price = range.low + range.range × level) from each completed daily, monthly, or Monday body range, then checks the next evaluation window (next day / next month / rest of that week respectively) for the first touch of each level — and measures whether price continued through it or reversed off it.
How to read this dashboard
Fade pips/touch
The average pip outcome of betting on the level reversing price, exiting at the eventual extreme. It's optimistic (no spread/slippage/perfect exit) — use it to compare slices against each other, not as a literal backtested return.
% Mean reversion
Of all touches of this level, the share where price reversed off it rather than continuing through. 50% = coin flip, no edge either way.
N (sample size)
How many times this level/day/session etc. was actually touched. Small N means the average is noisy — a single big winning or losing trade can swing it a lot. Shown as the orange line on the chart.
Significant? ("Sig")
A statistical test asking: "could this reversion rate just be random chance around 50/50?" Yes = under 5% chance it's noise (p<0.05) — the skew is probably real. No = plausibly just noise, especially if N is also small. A tall bar with "Sig = No" is the classic trap: looks exciting, isn't backed by enough data.
Confluence tightness
How close a daily level sits to a same-week Monday-range projected level. Tight = within a few pips (FX) / ~30 pips (gold); Regular = within ~10 pips (FX) / ~100 (gold); None = no nearby match.
Year range filter
Re-aggregates every chart/table live from the underlying yearly counts — not just a cosmetic slice. Use it to check whether an edge held up recently or was a one-off period.
