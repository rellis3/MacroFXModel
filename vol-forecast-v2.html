Vol & Range Forecaster — Feature Guide
Instrument Cards — Core Forecast

Each card is one instrument's daily vol forecast. All values are percentages of price (e.g. 0.65% means 65 pips on a 10,000-pip pair).

Vol %Today's estimated 1-day volatility (annualised ÷ √252). This is σ — the engine behind all range forecasts.
H-L Median / 75thExpected high-low range for the full day. Median = 50% of days historically exceed this. 75th = wider target reached ~25% of days.
O-C Median / 75thExpected close-to-open move (directional). Same percentile logic. Use for stop/target sizing when you have a directional bias.
O-H / O-LHow far price is expected to travel from the open upside (O-H) or downside (O-L). Same magnitude as O-C by Brownian motion reflection principle.
5-day / 20-dayMulti-day range scaled by √N (random walk). Useful for swing and position sizing.
Vol Percentile pillWhere today's σ sits in the trailing 252-day distribution. Red = top quartile (high vol), green = bottom quartile (quiet). Grey = mid-range.
News chip 📅Shown when a high-impact US event is detected today (FOMC ×1.35, NFP ×1.30, CPI ×1.25, PCE ×1.20, GDP/PPI ×1.15). The multiplier is applied to σ before computing ranges, so all forecasts on that row already reflect the expected event expansion.
Vol Cone Strip & Vol-of-Vol Badge

Shown on each card beneath the percentile pill. Answers "is vol expanding or contracting?" and "how reliable is this forecast?"

5d / 21d / 63d / 252dPercentile of today's σ inside each lookback window. If 5d > 252d the short-term is above its own history — vol is expanding (green). If 5d < 252d vol is contracting (amber). Flat = no regime signal.
Vol Stable / Moderate / UnstableVol-of-vol: coefficient of variation of the last 20 daily σ readings. Stable (<10% CV) = forecasts are reliable. Unstable (>20% CV) = σ is jumping day-to-day and the range estimate has wider uncertainty.
Session Tracker (inside each card)

The intraday progress bar and reach-time stamps track how far today's price action has moved against the forecast levels in real time.

Consumed barHow much of the median H-L forecast has been used up so far today. Green dot at the median mark, amber at the 75th. Fills left-to-right as range builds.
Breakout probabilityBrownian motion estimate of whether price will extend further given how much range is left and how much of the day has passed. High early in the day = range is not yet "used up". Falls as the session progresses and range is consumed.
Level reach timesThe UTC time each forecast level was first hit today: O-H med/75th (upside), O-L med/75th (downside), H-L med/75th (full range). Blank = not yet reached.
Setup Score (0–8)

Shown in the 📋 Levels table. A quick synthesis of conditions favouring a tradeable move today.

+2 Vol expanding5-day cone above 63-day cone — short-term vol above medium-term average.
+1 VoV stableVol-of-vol is Stable — forecast is reliable, not noisy.
+1 Weekly biasWTD pair direction aligns with the broader weekly trend.
+2 Range unconsumedLess than 40% of the median H-L has been consumed — most of the day's move is still ahead.
+2 Session activeCurrent London/NY session hours are within the historically high-activity window.
Currency Strength Strip

Shown across the top of the card grid. Aggregates the week-to-date directional bias of all cross pairs to score each major currency (EUR, USD, GBP, JPY, AUD, NZD, CHF, CAD) from bull to bear. Helps identify which side of a pair has momentum when sizing a directional trade.

📊 Hit Rates — Historical Level Reach Statistics

Answers: "on what % of days does price actually reach each forecast level, and how quickly?"

Hit rate %Fraction of past trading days on which that level was reached (from stored session audits). Higher = more reliable level for entries/targets.
Median reach timeUTC time at which the level is typically first hit on days it is reached. Tells you when to expect the move during the session.
Earliest / LatestThe earliest and latest the level has been hit across the sample — range of when to watch for it.

Data accumulates automatically as each day's session is saved. The more days in the archive, the more reliable the hit rates.

📊 Profile — Intraday Vol Profile

Bar chart of which UTC hours historically contribute the most to daily range, computed from 5 years of H1 Oanda data. Each bar = that hour's average contribution as a % of the full-day H-L. Use it to time entries: high bars = high-activity hours where levels are typically reached fastest.

Requires Session Stats to have been computed (click ⟳ Session Stats if bars are empty).

📅 Events — Event Vol Impact Study

Shows how much actual range expanded vs the base forecast on historical high-impact US event days (CPI, NFP, FOMC, PCE, GDP, PPI). Built from your own stored session audits cross-referenced against the Finnhub economic calendar.

×1.25 n=8On CPI days, actual range averaged 1.25× the forecast. Based on 8 stored event days in your archive.

Click ↻ Compute to run the study. Gets more accurate as your session audit archive grows. The current-day news multiplier (applied to today's forecast) is derived from this data.

📌 Ref Data — Reference Forecast Storage

Paste your external reference forecast export here and hit 💾 Save. It is stored in KV alongside our forecast so both are always available regardless of conversation resets. Pick any past date with the date picker to load a previously saved reference. Used for ongoing calibration: compare reference vs our export to identify systematic gaps and correct them.

⟳ Session Stats — Recompute Session Consumption

Fetches 5 years of H1 bars from Oanda for all instruments and computes what fraction of the daily H-L range each session (Asia 00:00–06:00, London 08:00–13:00, NY 13:00–21:00 London time) historically consumes. Takes 3–5 minutes. Results are stored in KV and used by the Intraday Profile panel. Run this once, or again after significant market regime changes.

Methodology Notes
Gold (commodity)Rogers-Satchell EWMA (λ=0.94) on daily OHLC. Handles trending markets without understating vol — unlike close-to-close estimators it doesn't subtract the directional component.
Indices (NQ, SPX, DE30…)GARCH(1,1): α=0.06, β=0.91, ω=4.76e-6 (~20% long-run floor). Responds quickly to vol spikes (α), mean-reverts over ~23 days (β), and can't collapse in quiet patches (ω floor).
FX pairsGARCH(1,1): same α/β, ω=3.60e-7 (~5% long-run floor). Lower floor reflects calmer FX baseline vs equity indices.
Range distributionBrownian motion: H-L P50 = 1.572σ, P75 = 2.049σ. O-C P50 = 0.675σ (half-normal). Per-asset correction factors calibrated against reference system.
Multi-day scalingσ_N = σ_1 × √N (independent days). Not ×N — range partially cancels across days for a random walk.
